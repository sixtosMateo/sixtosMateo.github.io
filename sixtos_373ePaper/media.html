The majority of news articles seem to look bad upon music piracy. For example in “Music Piracy is down but still very much,” Faughnder states “music industry is still trying to recover from piracy's heyday. Last year, total music industry revenue was about $15 billion worldwide, well below the 1999 peak of $38 billion.” This seems bad for business as apple considers music piracy as competition for their streaming music services development and deployment. In “What Is Reasonable?” there was a study that demonstrates people opinion about music piracy/music file sharing of different nations. It states “Few Americans with music files think it is “reasonable” to upload copies to websites where anyone can download them (15%), post links to “pirated copies” on websites such as Facebook (10%), or sell unauthorized copies (4%). Overall numbers are similar for TV/movies but differ in the age distribution. Significantly younger people (18–29) view sharing, uploading, and linking to TV/movie files as reasonable—especially uploading (24%) and linking to unauthorized videos (22%). ” The results show that less than half think that it is okay to share files in general even though some of them got their files from a hosting website.
